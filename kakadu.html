Kakadu National Park

Located in the Northern Territory,Kakadu National Park covers more than 19,840 square kilometers, making it the largest national park in Australia and the second largest in the world.

Within its borders lie monsoon rainforests, mangrove swamps, rivers, gorges, ancient rock paintings, wetlands, and waterfalls, as well as more than 300 different species of birds.

Precaution

Note that during the wet season (Nov-April), many roads and attractions close due to heavy flooding. Also, please be croc-wise.
